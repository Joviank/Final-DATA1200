Top 10 Ways Universal Design Can Help to Live a Longer and Happier Life.

Universal design is a tool that sheds light on creating technology for a helpful and user-friendly experience for everyone. This comes in handy for people with various disabilities and for those who are older. Here are some examples of universal designs that can help create a better and easier everyday life for the elderly. Many older people have problems with muscles and joints that make it difficult to move as they once did. That is why it is very important to have products such as sliding doors, lifts, stairlifts, and electric wheelchairs. These are all examples of products of universal design that can be used by everyone, but that may be necessary for some. For the elderly, it is important to be able to get both in and out of a building by using sliding doors, especially if you are in a wheelchair, it is also important that the wheelchair can be used even if you do not have mobility in your arms. Elevators are great for getting to your desired floor, even if you are in a wheelchair, have poor eyesight, or have poor hearing. You will also find lifts in the home, such as stairlifts. It acts as a chair that leads the person safely up to the floor of their own home. Many older people, for example, struggle with their hearing, and then, for example, a hearing aid comes in handy. They also struggle with everyday tasks, for example, and then google home and, for example, robot vacuum cleaners can be good to have. These devices help to automate tasks in the home so that the elderly can avoid doing them. A PC or mobile phone can be helpful in that you can stay up to date through online newspapers or be able to keep in good contact with family through phone, SMS, and email. Electric beds also help by making it easier to get in and out of bed. It can also make sleep better at better position.
